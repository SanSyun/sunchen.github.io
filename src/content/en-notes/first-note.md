## Core idea

A good learning note does more than summarize a tutorial. It captures how you think.

That usually means writing one of the following:

- a concept explained in your own words
- a comparison between two technical options
- a debugging record with concrete evidence
- a recap of tradeoffs you discovered while building something

## A simple template

When you write a note, this structure often works well:

1. What problem or question am I addressing?
2. What did I investigate?
3. What evidence changed my understanding?
4. What conclusion would I use in a real project?

## Why recruiters care

These notes make your profile stronger because they show:

- you can communicate clearly
- you understand technical nuance
- you can learn independently
- you create reusable knowledge
